# Channel Strategy — Single, Dual, or Multi-Channel

Encodes Ed Lawrence's dual-channel framework + Iman Gadzhi's three-channel model. Determines which channel a script targets *before* writing — that one input changes voice, depth, narrative intensity, hook tone, and CTA aggression.

Refer to this BEFORE filling out the context prompt at Step 5 of the main runbook.

---

## When to use the dual-channel model

The dual-channel model solves a specific failure mode: **a single channel that grew on broad content, now trying to convert buyers with narrow content, and the algo punishing the contradiction.**

YouTube tests every new video against your regular viewers first. If broad regulars don't click on narrow content, the algo stops pushing the video — and it never reaches the niche audience it was built for. The result: narrow content underperforms, broad content stops feeling on-brand, and the channel stalls.

The fix: split the conflict.
- **Channel 1 (Main / Growth):** broad-appeal content. Optimised for views. Top of funnel.
- **Channel 2 (Buyer / Conversion):** niche operator-tier content. Optimised for sales/leads. Bottom of funnel.

### Eligibility for dual-channel (Ed's hard criteria)

Don't run two channels unless ALL of these are true:

| Criterion | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Views per video | 1,000+ consistently |
| Subs OR equivalent following | 10k+ subs OR large social following |
| Time | Genuinely have it (not aspirational) |
| Existing audience to seed | Yes (otherwise you have nothing to send to Channel 2) |

If any of these fail → stay single-channel until they don't.

## The four Channel 2 strategies (pick 1-2, not all)

### Strategy 1 — Beginner → Advanced split (Iman Gadzhi Business)
- Main = broad / beginner-friendly / view-optimised
- Channel 2 = advanced strategy + deep system breakdowns + diagrams/frameworks
- Best for: creators whose offer targets more advanced audiences than the average viewer in their main niche
- Production: framework or diagram on screen as the visual spine. Unscripted-but-structured presenting. Cut waffle, no edit gymnastics.

### Strategy 2 — Fan content (Nick Saraev)
- Channel 2 = behind-the-scenes / personal / lessons / current-events reactions / wins+failures
- Best for: building deep audience trust + repeat-buyer loyalty
- Production: short (4-5 min), screen share or selfie cam, light editing, no thumbnails-as-art
- Stay relevant to niche — don't let "personal" drift into off-topic life advice

### Strategy 3 — Industry news + commentary (Neil Patel + Eric Siu)
- Channel 2 = reacting to breaking news in your space + commentary on what's happening now
- Best for: thought leadership + audiences who want to stay current
- Production: often podcast-style with a co-host, light edit, frequent updates
- Requires a reliable source of "what's happening" — RSS, Twitter, newsletter habit

### Strategy 4 — Content clipping + Q&A (Alex Hormozi)
- Channel 2 = clips of long-form content + Q&A answers to specific buyer questions
- Best for: high-volume creators serving busy people who want one-question-one-video
- Production: minimal — just clip, simple title stating the question, "anti-thumbnail" (still frame, not designed)
- Titles state the question directly — no curiosity gap. The viewer already has the question and is searching for it.

### Pick rule

- Most operators ship Strategy 1 + 2 mixed (advanced framework videos + occasional personal/behind-scenes).
- Strategy 3 needs a co-host or a reliable beat — skip if neither is available.
- Strategy 4 needs a long-form back catalog — viable for established creators, premature for new ones.

Ship 1 + 2 for the first 90 days, evaluate, then layer in 3 or 4.

## How channel choice changes the script

Same topic + same person + different channel → different script. Here's what shifts:

| Dimension | Channel 1 (Main / Growth) | Channel 2 (Buyer / Conversion) |
|---|---|---|
| **Audience** | Broad — anyone in the macro-niche | Narrow — defined ICP (specific revenue tier, role, or stage) |
| **Title formula** | Tactic-led, broad benefit, often number/result + tool | "How I [specific result] for [specific ICP]" — qualifiers IN the title |
| **Hook (intro)** | Title-mirror + cognitive-dissonance/question/fact hook | Title-mirror + buyer-credibility signal (named clients, dollar numbers) |
| **Depth** | Surface-to-medium — assume the viewer is new to the topic | Medium-to-deep — assume the viewer knows the basics, wants the operator-grade detail |
| **Narratives** | Attention + democratization heavily; light validation | Validation heavily (named clients, real results); democratization within the ICP only |
| **Examples** | Broadly relatable cases; light specificity | Specific dollar numbers, named clients, real timelines |
| **Production** | View-optimised — strong thumbnail, hook-driven, edited tight | Anti-design OK — frameworks, diagrams, simple thumbnails, "boring" titles can win |
| **Length** | Whatever the algo rewards (manual trim) | Long-form welcome — buyers will watch 30-40 min if value is high |
| **CTA** | Soft / no CTA — top of funnel, capture via subscribe + newsletter | Explicit CTA — book a call / opt-in / specific next step (the buyer is ready) |
| **Tracking metric** | Views + impressions CTR + sub conversion | Calls booked + email signups + revenue per view |

## Niche flooding (advanced, optional)

Once Channel 1 + Channel 2 are running, a third move is to **niche flood** — run multiple channels in the same niche to deny competitors traction. Used by faceless content factories; applicable to personal brands too.

Mechanism: the more impressions in your niche that go to channels you own, the fewer go to competitors who'd take your audience. Done seriously this means 3-5+ channels covering different angles of the same niche.

Only viable if Channel 1 + 2 are already operating cleanly + the operator has capacity. Most creators stop at 2.

## Tracking discipline

Without tracking, you can't tell which channel/strategy is working. Track per video:

| Metric | Where to record |
|---|---|
| Views | Studio analytics (already collected) |
| Email signups attributed to the video | Spreadsheet or per-video unique tracking link |
| Calls booked attributed | Spreadsheet from booking tool, tagged by video |
| Sales generated attributed | CRM, tagged by source video |
| Revenue per view | Sales attributed ÷ views |
| Link clicks per view | Studio's click data on description links |

Use a simple Google Sheet or Notion table — one row per video, columns for the metrics above. Update weekly. Tools like RevTrack productize this; the manual version works fine if you're disciplined.

**The goal of tracking:** at the 90-day mark, compare the two channels' revenue-per-view and total leads/sales. If Channel 2 is generating more business than Channel 1's narrow-content used to → keep both running. If not → shut Channel 2 and put everything on Channel 1.

## Decision tree before scripting

```
1. Does Lauren / the user meet dual-channel eligibility? (1k views, 10k+ subs, time, existing audience)
   - No → single channel. Pick Main / Business / Extended persona from narratives-and-channels.md and proceed.
   - Yes → continue ↓

2. Is this video for Channel 1 (broad/views) or Channel 2 (narrow/conversion)?
   - Channel 1 → use broad audience profile + view-optimised hook + soft/no CTA
   - Channel 2 → continue ↓

3. Which Channel 2 strategy does this video fit?
   - Strategy 1 (beginner→advanced): heavy framework + diagram, narrow ICP, 30-40 min OK
   - Strategy 2 (fan): short (4-5 min), behind-scenes, personal lesson, screen-share
   - Strategy 3 (news/commentary): topical, reactive, podcast-style if applicable
   - Strategy 4 (clipping/Q&A): one question, anti-thumbnail, simple title

4. What's the explicit ICP qualifier? Lock it before drafting the title.
   - e.g. "for coaches doing $100k–$1M/mo"
   - e.g. "for agency owners scaling past $50k/mo"
   - The title MUST contain or strongly imply this qualifier

5. Proceed to context prompt, with channel + strategy + ICP qualifier filled in.
```
