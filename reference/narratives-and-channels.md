# Channels & Narratives

## The 3 channels (Iman / Educate.io reference model)

Each channel has a different persona and tone. The channel choice changes outline depth, language register, narrative intensity, and CTA usage.

### Main Channel — broad appeal
- **Viewer:** Mix of beginners and intermediates exploring online business / personal finance / making money. May not know the unique mechanism yet.
- **Tone:** Accessible, transformation-focused, energetic.
- **Narrative work:** Full transformation framework — attention + validation + democratization + timing all instilled subtly through arguments and proof.
- **Use case:** Warm-up videos before a lead-capture period; videos in the lead-capture period with explicit CTAs to the event/VSL/challenge.
- **Format example for shorter videos (10–15 min):** Conversational, off-the-dome, often filmed in unconventional settings (e.g. Iman's car). Iman's preferred deliverable for these = **bullet points**, not a word-for-word teleprompter.

### Business Channel — sophisticated audience
- **Viewer:** Established coaches / advisors / agency owners / $100k+/mo founders. They want high-leverage strategy, not introductory content.
- **Tone:** Restrained, peer-to-peer, technical.
- **Narrative work:** Light. The viewer is past the initial belief-installation stage.
- **Format:** Heavily visual — Canva slides, Miro boards as overlays. Speaks AND shows simultaneously so the viewer can follow both auditorily and visually.

### Extended Channel — smart beginners
- **Viewer:** Already found the brand, watched the main channel, knows the unique mechanism, knows what specialised AI is. Wants more nuanced, step-by-step depth.
- **Tone:** Goodwill + nurturing primary; teaching secondary.
- **Narrative work:** Reduced intensity vs Main. BUT still subtly reinforce **unique-mechanism narratives** (Digital Products 2.0 + Specialised AI, or whichever the brand's positioning is) so positioning stays consistent across the ecosystem.
- **Format:** Longer 30–40 min technical deep-dives are common here.

## The 4 narratives (warm-up framework)

These narratives apply *primarily to Main Channel content* — especially warm-up campaigns and video sequences that lead toward a VSL or offer. They engineer beliefs into the viewer's mind without explicitly stating them. Woven through arguments, examples, and proof.

### 1. Attention narrative
- **Mechanism:** Make the viewer feel they're discovering something hidden in plain sight that most people are walking past.
- **Psychological effect:** Positions them as "early adopters who see what others don't" — opposite of "late to the party."
- **In script:** Frame the opportunity as something most people don't recognise yet. Tone of "you're seeing this before everyone else does."

### 2. Validation narrative
- **Mechanism:** Show that credible, successful people are already doing this.
- **Psychological effect:** Removes the risk of being wrong via social proof. Reduces decision anxiety. "If respected people are doing it, this isn't a foolish move — it's what smart people do."
- **In script:** Name actual coaches, brands, or proof-figures already on the path. Specific names beat vague "many entrepreneurs."

### 3. Democratization narrative
- **Mechanism:** Remove the capability barrier. Show they don't need years of expertise, special skills, or unique advantages.
- **Psychological effect:** Transforms "this works for other people but not for me" → "this is specifically designed to work for someone in my exact situation."
- **In script:** Use examples that resemble the viewer's own context. Show the unique mechanism's enabler (e.g. specialised AI) makes accessibility real. Make it sound easy without making it sound trivial.

### 4. Timing narrative
- **Mechanism:** Explain why NOW is the moment — through genuine market conditions, technology shifts, or competitive dynamics that create a temporary window.
- **Psychological effect:** Urgency without manipulation. The window is real, not artificial scarcity.
- **In script:** Bitcoin in 2012 is the canonical example. Reference current shifts (e.g. AI capability jumps, platform changes, market gaps) that won't last forever.

## Mechanism & contributor

This is the architecture of what's being positioned across the entire campaign:

- **Unique mechanism** = the core "thing" being positioned (e.g. *Digital Products 2.0 = Digital Products + AI*).
- **Contributor / enabler** = the layer that makes the unique mechanism possible or accessible to the viewer (e.g. *Specialised AI tools*).

Every video in a sprint reinforces both. The unique mechanism is the offer-adjacent positioning. The contributor is what makes democratization narratively true.

The script never says "this is our unique mechanism." It demonstrates the mechanism through examples, shows the contributor as what makes it work, and leaves the viewer believing the conclusion themselves.
