## The Universal 4-Beat Intro (validated across all 7)

Every verified outlier opens with a 4-beat skeleton in the first ~30-60 seconds. This is more granular than the 3-line Boring Intro System constraint — the 3-line version compresses these 4 beats into 3 sentences. Both work; the 4-beat version is what the actual transcripts show.

```
BEAT 1 — Title-mirror or proof claim (0-8 sec)
  Confirms the viewer is in the right video. Often includes a number/result.

BEAT 2 — Hook (8-15 sec)
  Named mechanism / contrarian frame / counter-intuitive reveal / 
  reassurance disarm. Spikes curiosity above the title.

BEAT 3 — Promise (15-25 sec)
  Concrete outcome viewer gets by watching. Often includes timeframe + scope.

BEAT 4 — Credibility (25-45 sec)
  Why listen to YOU. Dollar floor, named clients, named tool, scale data.
```

## Annotated examples — exactly how each outlier deploys the 4 beats

### Saraev 9.8x (long-form course, $4M/yr authority)

**BEAT 1:** *"Hey, this is the definitive course on Claude Code for beginners."*
- Title mirror: "definitive course" → "FULL COURSE"
- Audience filter: "for beginners"

**BEAT 4 (early — credibility front-loaded):** *"I use Claude Code every day to manage a business that does over $4 million a year in profit. I also teach over 2,000 people how to use Claude Code both for personal and professional tasks."*
- Dollar floor: $4M/yr
- Named teaching credential: 2,000 students

**BEAT 2 + 3 (compressed):** *"Once you understand what I'm about to show you in this course, it's no small stretch to say that Claude Code will augment your productivity. You'll gain leverage in areas that you probably didn't even realize that you had."*
- Promise: leverage gains
- Hook (subtle): "areas you didn't realize you had"

**Why credibility-first works for long-form courses:** viewers are committing 4 hours. They need credibility before promise.

### Tom Youngs 3.7x (long-form $20k+/m course)

**BEAT 1 + 3 (fused):** *"In today's video, I'm showing you behind the curtains of exactly how I've built my micro education business over the past three years from zero to having my first seven figure year last year."*
- Title mirror: "$20k+/m coaching offer" → "micro education business... seven figure year"
- Promise: "behind the curtains... exactly how"
- Transformation arc: 0 → 7-figures in 3 years

**BEAT 2 (Free-asset giveaway hook):** *"I'm giving you absolutely everything. The strategies, the playbooks, the workbooks, the GPTs, the spreadsheets, literally everything that I've built and given to my clients."*
- B10 formula in action: free-asset list-stack

**BEAT 4 (named-product credibility):** *"...in my consulting and advisory business, Scale 20, where I help six-figure online educators scale to seven figures per year."*
- Named system: "Scale 20"
- ICP definition: "six-figure online educators"
- Outcome scope: "seven figures"

### Cole Gordon 3.7x ($1M Personal Brand)

**Cole's pattern is different — opens with BORROWED CELEBRITY CREDIBILITY (Dan Koe interview clip).**

**BEAT 4 (borrowed via Dan Koe quote):** *"Dan, you have 1.3 million on YouTube, 800K on X, 1.7 on IG... and you do it all in like two hours a day."*
- Borrowed credibility = celebrity-tier creator validates the framework before Cole says a word.
- This is the **interview-as-intro pattern** — uses someone else's authority to bypass the "why should I listen to you" question.

**Use case:** when you have access to celebrity-tier guests + want their credibility to lend weight before your own framework.

### Martell 5.0x (You're Not Behind, 18 min)

This is the CLEANEST 4-beat outlier — exactly matches the Boring Intro System.

**BEAT 1 (Title-mirror + reassurance):** *"Believe it or not, you're not behind on AI yet."*
- Mirrors the title verbatim.
- Reassurance hook (B7 formula).

**BEAT 2 (Break-belief):** *"Because the truth is, most people still don't understand how to use it well."*
- Counter-conventional-wisdom: "I'm behind" → "no, most people don't actually know."

**BEAT 3 (Promise + retention loop):** *"By just watching this video, you'll know more about AI than 90% of the people out there. And if you make it all the way till the end, you'll be in the top 1% of all AI users."*
- 90% / 1% concrete specificity.
- Retention hook embedded: "if you make it all the way till the end..."

**BEAT 4 (Credibility):** *"I've personally tested thousands of AI prompts across every major platform, built dozens of custom AI tools for my own companies, and helped hundreds of founders turn AI into their most profitable team members."*
- Scale data: thousands / dozens / hundreds.
- Authority frame: "most profitable team members" (operator-language).

**This is the canonical 4-beat outlier structure.** Lauren's videos should mirror this beat order.

### Tom @AI Growth Lab 33x (Twitter Followers)

**BEAT 1 (Result claim):** *"I grew my Twitter account to over 15,000 followers, hitting over 7.7 million impressions, and Twitter paid me $2,800."*

**BEAT 2 (Counter-intuitive reveal hook):** *"...just for posting content that I didn't even write myself."*
- "Didn't even write myself" = the contrarian hook (B9 formula adjacent).

- Named mechanism: "the hybrid method."

**BEAT 3 continued (concrete outcome):** *"I'm going to show you the exact workflow that generates high quality viral tweets 10 times faster than manual writing and way cheaper than hiring a ghostwriter."*
- Comparison framing: "10x faster" / "cheaper than ghostwriter."

**Pattern note:** highest outlier ratio (33x) of any tested. Three-stack of numbers + counter-intuitive reveal + named mechanism + comparison — all in ~25 seconds.

### Nicky Saunders 9.6x (4 AI Teams Claude Cowork)

- This is the section-level Value Loop = nested Value Loops. Each section is its own video-within-a-video.

## Fill-in templates for Lauren — by video type

### Template A — Long-form course (Saraev / Tom Youngs pattern)

For: methodology deep-dives, full guides, comprehensive walkthroughs.

```
Intro:
Hey, this is [the definitive guide / full course / complete deep dive] on 
[NAMED METHODOLOGY] for [SPECIFIC ICP].

I [run / built / scaled] [PROOF — your own business or Foundr / named client]. 
[Dollar floor or scale data].

[Authority claim — "I've taught X people / installed for X clients..."]

[Promise — what they'll get by watching the full thing.]

[Free-asset giveaway list — "I'm giving you everything: the templates, the 
playbooks, the prompts, the spreadsheets..."]

[Section structure preview — "First we'll cover X, then Y, then Z."]
```

### Template B — Time-bound mastery (Martell pattern, B7 reassurance + low-time)

For: skill-building videos with explicit timeframe promise.

```
Intro:
Believe it or not, you're not [behind / late / too far] on [TOPIC] yet. 
Because the truth is, most people still don't understand how to [do X] well.

By just watching this video, you'll know more about [TOPIC] than [X%] 
of the people out there. And if you make it all the way to the end, 
you'll be in the top [Y%].

I've personally [SPECIFIC PROOF: tested thousands of X / built dozens of Y / 
helped hundreds of Z]. So today I'm going to show you [PROMISE in 1 sentence].
```

### Template C — Personal-result case study (Tom @AGL 33x pattern)

For: "how I did X" videos with named mechanism.

```
Intro:
I [grew / made / built] [SPECIFIC RESULT — three-stack of numbers]. 

[Counter-intuitive reveal — "...without even doing the obvious thing"].

The entire system uses [NAMED TOOL] with what I call [NAMED MECHANISM], 
combining [X] with [Y].

I'm going to show you the exact [workflow / system / template] that 
[CONCRETE OUTCOME] [N times faster / cheaper / better than the alternative].
```

### Template D — Fast utility tutorial (Nicky / Patrick pattern)

For: do-this-then-this tactical screen recordings.

```
Intro (no credibility — straight into utility):
Right now [I'm doing X / I have Y on my screen / this is the result of Z]. 
[Title-mirror with specific scope]. [Identity bracket — "no employees / no team / no coding"].

This is [NAMED TOOL].

By the end of this video, you're going to [CONCRETE PROMISE — "know exactly 
the same setup that I do" / "have the same system running"].

[Go directly into screen recording — no further preamble.]
```

### Template E — Contrarian / negative hook (Patrick Dang pattern)

For: industry-critique videos that establish authenticity through opposition.

```
Intro:
I'm seeing all these [people / creators / experts] say [conventional claim], 
but after watching their videos, I realized they don't even do what they 
teach. [They're only doing it for views / to sell you something].

So in this video, I'm going to [share / show / break down] realistically 
how I personally [DO THE THING] — [authenticity claim].

[Scope tease: "All the way from X, to Y, and even Z."]
```

### Template F — Borrowed celebrity credibility (Cole Gordon pattern)

For: when you have access to celebrity-tier guests.

```
Intro:
[OPEN WITH GUEST QUOTE/CLIP that contains the proof claim or framework reveal.]

[Brief pivot: "What I want to break down is..."]

[Now your own promise + scope.]

[Goes into dialog/teach format.]
```

## Quality gate — match against this template before delivery

Before any intro ships:

- [ ] Beat 1 — title-mirror or proof claim with specific numbers (not vague)
- [ ] Beat 2 — hook (named mechanism / contrarian / counter-intuitive / reassurance)
- [ ] Beat 3 — promise (concrete outcome viewer gets by watching)
- [ ] Beat 4 — credibility (or skipped if fast-utility tutorial — show-don't-tell)
- [ ] Total length within Boring Intro System cap (3 short lines / 8 sec for short videos, 30-45 sec for long-form courses)
- [ ] Title Case for YouTube (NOT lowercase LinkedIn rule)
- [ ] Mirrors one of Templates A-F or hybrid combination
- [ ] Named tool / named mechanism / named system used (not generic "AI")
- [ ] Dollar floor or specific number in Beat 1 or 4
- [ ] No invented system names — use existing (Content Engine / DM Sales Engine / SCALE / Writing Flywheel)

If any check fails → rewrite the intro before drafting any other section.
