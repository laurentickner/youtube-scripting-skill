# A/B Testing Protocol — Thumbnails + Titles

*Sourced from Lauren's YouTube consultant. Locked into skill 2026-05-02.*

The A/B test is what turns a 1-in-10 outlier into a 3-in-10 outlier. Without testing, you ship with your best guess. With testing, you ship with the algorithm's verdict.

---

## Required Chrome extensions

- **VidIQ** (free) — install before any competitor research. Shows outlier score per video on YouTube search results + channel pages. **5-10x outlier score = good model to take inspiration from.** Without VidIQ, you're guessing which videos broke out vs. which are baseline. Install: chrome web store → vidIQ.

## Tool: thumbnailtest.com (NOT TubeBuddy)

### Why thumbnailtest.com over TubeBuddy

| Capability | thumbnailtest.com | TubeBuddy |
|---|---|---|
| Hourly testing | ✅ | ❌ daily only |
| Multiple variations | ✅ unlimited | ❌ one-at-a-time |
| Bayesian analysis | ✅ probability-based | ❌ raw CTR only |
| **CTR × AVD metric** | ✅ avoids clickbait trap | ❌ CTR only |

**The CTR × AVD metric is the only one that matters.** A high-CTR thumbnail with low retention is clickbait — YouTube's algo eventually penalises it. The combined CTR × AVD score balances clickability AND viewer retention.

## First 24-48 hours rule (NEW UPLOADS)

**Hourly testing — strongly recommended.**

For new uploads, set up:
- 4-5 variations + original
- 1 hour per variation
- 4-5 hour total cycle
- Tests must run during peak audience hours (NOT 1am if your audience is daytime active)

**Critical timing trick:** thumbnailtest.com has a 1-hour buffer before tests start. To make testing begin at the right time:
- Want testing to start at 6pm? Publish video at 4:55pm + schedule test within those 5 minutes.
- Test will start at ~6pm (1 hour after publish), maximising the critical first-24h window.

If a clear winner emerges in the first 4-5 hours → end the test early + click "Set Thumbnail" on the winner. Gives YouTube a stable thumbnail to optimise distribution against.

## 2-6 week post-test signal — Browse + Suggested traffic

Per the consultant: *"After you test a video give it 2-6 weeks, keep an eye on the traffic source. Ideally, you should see an increase on browse and suggested sources."*

**What this means:**
- The first 24-48h tells you if packaging works (CTR signal)
- Weeks 2-6 tell you if YouTube is *actively pushing* the video to non-subscribers
- The metric to watch: **% of views from "Browse features" + "Suggested videos"** (in YT Studio Analytics → Reach → Traffic source types)

**The signal:**
- If Browse + Suggested % is **rising** week over week → YouTube's algo is finding the video its audience. The packaging + content combination is winning. Keep producing content like this.
- If Browse + Suggested % is **flat or falling** → algo isn't pushing it. Could be: audience-mismatch (wrong viewers being served), declining freshness, or fundamental packaging miss. Consider rehash.

This is a **slower signal than CTR** but a more reliable one for long-tail outliers (1-in-10 rule). A video can hit modest CTR in week 1 but compound via Browse + Suggested over months. Don't kill before 6 months.

## First-24h diagnostic rule

| Symptom | Issue | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Low CTR** in first 24h | Packaging (title + thumbnail) | Iterate immediately. Test new variations. |
| **High CTR + low AVD** | Content (intro / hook / pacing) | Don't blame thumbnail. Fix the video for next time. Don't iterate thumbnail — it's working. |
| **Low CTR + low AVD** | Topic mismatch | Likely wrong-audience pull. Privatise or unlist if it's polluting the channel. |
| **Low CTR + high AVD** | Packaging hides good content | Iterate packaging hard. Content is solid; packaging is failing it. |

## Older videos (REHASHES) — daily testing

**When to rehash:**
- Underperforming videos with good content
- Already-successful videos (small lift = big absolute)
- Videos with outdated visuals (old AI tools / old appearance / old year refs)

**Daily testing setup:**
- 3-5 new thumbnail variations + original
- 2-week minimum test
- Platform suggests duration based on # of variations (4 vars = ~16 days, 6 vars = ~20 days)

**Updating thumbnails on OLD videos — temporal consistency:**
- Use creator images that match the appearance in THAT video
- Don't use current images for very old videos (creates a disconnect when viewers click)
- Update text/graphics/background while maintaining temporal consistency

## Best practices (always)

- ✅ Always include the original as baseline (100% recommended)
- ✅ Test during peak audience hours
- ✅ Use Classic format (rotation A→B→C→D→A) for new uploads
- ✅ Use Consecutive format (A then B then C) for older videos
- ✅ Auto-set winner = OK but review CTR×AVD before committing
- ✅ Manually click "Set Thumbnail" once winner is decided
- ❌ Don't end early on rehashes — let full test run for reliable data
- ❌ Don't optimise for raw CTR alone — clickbait trap

---

## Strategic testing approach

### Sequential: thumbnails first, titles second

If the title is already solid + matches the video content (not clickbait):

1. **Test thumbnails first** — biggest CTR lever
2. If thumbnail variations don't beat the original AND video is still underperforming → **test titles**
3. If both fail individually → **test title + thumbnail combinations** (full repackage)

### Process of elimination

If you've tested various thumbnails and the original keeps winning, BUT video is underperforming → the thumbnail isn't the issue. Move to title testing.

## Title testing nuances

Two title-style approaches worth testing in 2026:

| Style | Example | When |
|---|---|---|
| Traditional | *"How to Make $10,000 With This Method in 2026"* | SEO-friendly, search-driven topics |
| Conversational | *"Give me 11 mins, I'll show you how to make $X/day with [topic] (2026)"* | Suggested-feed videos, personal-result framing |

For title rehashing: focus on already-well-performing videos to lift them further. Underperforming videos rarely save themselves with a title change alone.

---

## Iteration speed

Small tweaks = big CTR shifts. Things to change:

- Title angle
- Thumbnail emotion / concept
- Wording
- Framing
- Single element (face vs no-face / chart vs metric / etc.)

**Iterate fast. Don't tweak one variable per week — test 4-5 in the first 24h.**

---

## Outliers compound — the 1-in-10 rule

> *"I almost can say I know they're not gonna perform right off the bat. They're gonna be 1 of 10."*

- Outliers don't appear immediately. Don't expect every video to break out.
- 1 in 10 of your videos breaks out. The other 9 do their job (build catalog, signal niche, train algo).
- Quote: *"all the videos I've been getting like 100 views a week — now we're getting 10,000 views from it in 6 months."*

**Skill rule:** don't kill a video before 6 months unless you A/B-tested in the first 48hrs and it didn't lift. Long-tail accumulation is the engine.

---

## Channel maintenance cycle

Weekly:
- Review last 7 days of performance
- Identify candidates for rehash (declining videos with good content)
- Identify candidates for further optimisation (top performers that could go even bigger)

Monthly:
- Full channel audit — top videos, sub-conversion rate, traffic sources
- Test or rehash 1-2 candidates

Quarterly:
- Re-scan competitor outlier patterns (recent 90-day shifts)
- Update title-formulas.md if new patterns emerge
