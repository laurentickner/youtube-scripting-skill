# Packaging Workflow — Target → Pain → Title → Thumbnail

**This is the canonical packaging order. Apply BEFORE picking a title formula. Skipping any step = packaging fails the algorithm + the audience.**

---

## The 4-step order (non-negotiable)

```
Target viewer → Promise/Pain → Title → Thumbnail
```

Always this sequence. Never start with the title. Never start with the thumbnail. Both are downstream artifacts of the upstream decisions.

---

## Step 1 — Target viewer

**NOT a demographic. A specific constraint that kills half the bad angles.**

| ❌ Wrong (demographic) | ✅ Right (constraint) |
|---|---|
| "Business owners" | "Someone who has tried ChatGPT for LinkedIn posts and hated how it sounded" |
| "Online coaches" | "Coaches at $250k/yr stuck because their content team can't keep up" |
| "Founders" | "Founders who hired a marketing agency, paid $25k/mo, and got generic content" |

Why: the constraint is a filter. It auto-rejects angles that wouldn't land. It makes Step 2 (pain/promise) unambiguous.

How to derive: ask **"who is the EXACT person this video helps right now? What did they JUST try that didn't work?"** — that "just tried X and it didn't work" framing IS the target viewer.

## Step 2 — Promise / pain

**The pain comes from what they JUST tried that didn't work. The promise comes from what your video actually delivers.**

The pain is often LITERALLY the opening line of the video. If the video opens with *"your AI content sounds robotic"* — that IS the pain. Don't invent a different pain in the packaging step.

Format the output as two single sentences:
- **Pain:** "My [thing] is [broken state], and I'm stuck either [bad option A] or [bad option B]."
- **Promise:** "[Solution] that [transformation], in [timeframe], without [losing what matters]."

Example (AI Clone video):
- Pain: *"My AI content sounds generic and robotic, nobody engages with it, and I'm stuck either wasting hours writing manually or posting slop that flops."*
- Promise: *"An AI trained on YOU that writes posts indistinguishable from your real voice, a week of content in under an hour, without losing who you are."*

## Step 3 — Title

**Now you can pick a formula** from `reference/title-formulas.md`. Not before.

Process:
1. Check what's performing for OTHER creators in the niche (last-90-day outliers in `youtube-strategy/research-recent-outliers-90day.md`)
2. Pick the closest formula that fits the pain/promise
3. Tweak the formula to match THIS specific pain/promise
4. Char count <70

The opening line of the video often IS the title. If video opens with *"your AI content sounds robotic"*, the title can literally be:
- *"Your AI Sounds Robotic"*
- *"Stop Posting AI Slop"*
- *"Why ChatGPT Can't Write Like You"*

These are all forms of B9 (Negative Hook) tweaked to the specific pain.

**Rule:** if the title doesn't directly call out the pain identified in Step 2, rewrite it.

## Step 4 — Thumbnail

**See `reference/thumbnail-design.md` for full design framework.**

The packaging-step rule: the thumbnail must show **TANGIBLE PROOF** of the promise — readable in <1 second.

| ❌ Bad thumbnail | ✅ Good thumbnail |
|---|---|
| Logo stacks (fiverr / discord / shopify) | Real screenshot of the actual result (LinkedIn growth chart, dashboard, Stripe screenshot) |
| Product-descriptive text ("AI Content Engine for Coaches") | Cause label that names the mechanism ("AI CLONE") |
| Generic agency-style design | Face + concrete proof element + arrow to the inflection point |
| AI-generated thumbnail | Real photo, real screenshot |

The thumbnail isn't describing the product. It's **showing what happened when the viewer used it.**

Example (AI Clone video):
- Face on one side
- LinkedIn growth chart on the other side, big and crisp
- Arrow pointing to inflection point
- Short text label: "AI CLONE" or "AI COPY"
- Variant: before/after of LinkedIn insights dashboard, left flat, right millions of impressions

---

## Required deliverable format

When proposing packaging, output ALL FOUR steps explicitly. Don't skip any. Don't deliver a title without showing the upstream work:

```
TARGET VIEWER:
[Specific constraint sentence, not demographic]

PAIN:
[One sentence — what's broken + what bad options they're stuck choosing between]

PROMISE:
[One sentence — solution + transformation + timeframe + what's preserved]

TITLE (formula: [B/N number from title-formulas.md]):
[The title — under 70 chars, in Title Case for YouTube]

THUMBNAIL:
[1-2 sentence concept description — what's the visible proof element + face + label]
[Variant: alternative concept worth A/B testing]
```

Without all 4, the packaging is incomplete. Reject and rewrite.

---

## Common failures to catch

1. **Picking the title before the target viewer** — produces broad-appeal titles that don't filter audience. The Channel 1 collab needs a filter word for a reason.
2. **Inventing a pain that's not the opening line of the video** — disconnect between what the video says and what the title promises = poor AVD = algo penalty.
3. **Thumbnail describes the product instead of showing the result** — *"AI Content Engine"* on a thumbnail is product description; *"$100k/mo"* with a Stripe screenshot is proof.
4. **Generic constraint ("for coaches")** — Lauren's filter word "Online Coaches" is good but the per-video target viewer needs to be tighter (e.g. "online coaches who tried ChatGPT for content and gave up").
5. **Thumbnail face + text but no proof element** — face alone isn't proof. Face + chart / dashboard / before-after IS proof.

## Universal packaging rules (apply to every video)

### Mr Beast principle — packaging > idea
> *"You can have the best idea in the world, but if you package it wrong, it's not gonna perform. You can have a decent idea with great packaging that's gonna perform well."*

Implications:
- A great idea doesn't survive bad packaging.
- A decent idea + great packaging = win.
- Don't ship without good packaging even if you love the idea.
- Mr Beast banana example: "Why I Hate Bananas" vs "Why Bananas Are the Worst Food in the World" — same content, opposite outcomes.

### 1-in-10 outlier rule
> *"All the videos I've been getting like 100 views a week — now we're getting 10,000 views from it in 6 months."*

- Only 1 in 10 videos breaks out as an outlier.
- The other 9 do their job — build catalog, signal niche, train algo.
- Don't kill a video before 6 months unless first-48hr A/B testing failed.
- Long-tail accumulation is the engine. Many videos that look dead at week 1 hit 10k views by month 6.

### Redirect at the end of every video (no exceptions)
> *"You're not gonna do content for everyone for free."*

- Every video ends with a redirect: next-video / lead magnet / Channel 2 link / Inner Circle / DFY application.
- Tutorial-only channels attract beginner audience who never convert. Always pitch the next step.
- "How to → how to → how to" every time = beginner channel, kills funnel.
- Mix tutorials with non-tutorials. Funnel viewers through.

### LinkedIn / short-form ≠ YouTube
- A video that worked on LinkedIn or TikTok will NOT automatically work on YouTube without packaging rework.
- Short-form: video is FED to viewer (auto-play). Packaging matters less.
- YouTube: viewer ACTIVELY CLICKS. Packaging is everything.
- Don't port titles cross-platform. Re-package for YouTube.

### Series strategy (validated by consultant)
- "Replace [team]" topic is in-vogue in 2026. Validated as Lauren's right starting point.
- The marketing-team / sales-team / replaced-coaches series is the right opening sequence.
- Quote: *"How to use Claude is everywhere — the interesting bit is how can I use it in my business to replace a team, or reduce salaries, or reduce monthly costs."*

---

## Quality gate

Before delivering ANY packaging, audit it against this checklist:

- [ ] Target viewer is a specific constraint, not a demographic
- [ ] Pain is literally derivable from the video's opening lines
- [ ] Promise has a concrete transformation + timeframe + what's preserved
- [ ] Title formula ID is cited (B# / N# from title-formulas.md)
- [ ] Title under 70 chars
- [ ] Title Case (YouTube), not lowercase (LinkedIn)
- [ ] Thumbnail concept shows tangible proof, not product description
- [ ] Thumbnail readable in <1 second
- [ ] Thumbnail face + emotion matches the title's emotional valence

If any fail → rewrite that step. Don't deliver the packaging.
